Executive Synopsis

The Business Problem: Expert Validation Is Inaccessible to European Startups

Every year, European B2B startups waste ‚Ç¨500M+ building products for buyers they've never spoken to. A healthcare software founder spends 8 months developing features for "hospital IT departments," only to discover‚Äîafter launch‚Äîthat hospital procurement actually happens at the network level, not individual facilities. Those 8 months and ‚Ç¨200K in development costs are gone.

Traditional expert validation could have prevented this. Management consultants, advisory boards, and market research firms can tell founders exactly what enterprise buyers need. But at ‚Ç¨5K-50K per engagement, early-stage startups simply cannot afford it.

The Masters platform solves this cost barrier: We connect B2B startup founders directly with enterprise decision-makers‚Äîthe hospital CIOs, procurement directors, and department heads who actually buy software‚Äîfor live validation interviews at 1/10th traditional consulting costs.

Our model:

  • Startups submit their value propositions
  • The Masters (enterprise buyers/decision-makers) review submissions

That AI doesn't exist yet. Current AI systems fail at this task in two critical ways, and solving these failures is the focus of this R&D grant.

Flaw #2: Context Collapse‚ÄîCatastrophic Knowledge Loss Over Time

As AI systems conduct more interviews, their performance paradoxically gets worse, not better. Performance drops sharply as critical details erode through repeated summarization‚Äîa phenomenon we call "context collapse."

The core challenge: How can AI learn from 1,000 expert interviews if it forgets the most valuable insights from the first 10?

For The Masters to work, the AI must become MORE knowledgeable with every interview, not less. A system that forgets insights cannot scale expert validation.

EU Strategic Relevance: Solving this problem has implications far beyond The Masters. Context collapse is the fundamental reason 95% of enterprise AI deployments fail. Businesses cannot afford continuous fine-tuning, yet current context methods lose critical knowledge. This research addresses a barrier to EU-wide AI adoption.

‚úÖ Our Solution: Agentic Context Engineering (ACE)

ACE eliminates both fine-tuning dependency and context collapse through a fundamentally different approach: instead of compressing knowledge, we preserve everything and build an evolving playbook that grows with every interaction.

The breakthrough: ACE-augmented small models (7-13B parameters like Llama-2, Mistral) match or exceed GPT-4's performance on complex reasoning tasks while consuming 95% less computational resources and reducing inference costs by two orders of magnitude.

Technical validation:

  • 10.6% performance improvement over baseline approaches
  • 86.9% lower adaptation latency compared to fine-tuning
  • Zero knowledge degradation over time (solves context collapse)

üéØ Focus of the R&D Work: Three Research Pillars

This grant will fund critical R&D to bridge the gap between existing context adaptation methods (which work well for structured tasks like code generation) and the highly complex domain of live, voice-based Socratic interviews with human experts.

Why this is novel: Current ACE research focuses on structured domains where success/failure is binary‚Äîcode either runs or crashes, API calls either succeed or fail. Human conversation is fundamentally different: success is qualitative, context-dependent, and measured in subtle signals like hesitation, tone shifts, and "aha" moments.
üî¨ Three Research Pillars
1
Execution Feedback for Live Conversation
Novel Challenge:
Existing methods rely on binary feedback (test passed/failed). Our system must identify subtle, qualitative signals during voice conversations: founder defensiveness, hesitation patterns, breakthrough moments, expert engagement levels.
Expected Output:
Train the Reflector agent to score conversation quality based on qualitative human signals‚Äîturning subjective assessment into quantitative feedback for the self-improvement loop.

Technical Approach: Multi-modal analysis (voice tone, speech patterns, pause length), conversation outcome metrics, longitudinal tracking of founder success.
2
Building a Playbook for Socratic Dialogue
Novel Challenge:
Current ACE research curates concrete artifacts: code snippets, API rules, database schemas. We need to curate effective conversational strategies: question sequencing, assumption-revealing techniques, context-switching patterns, founder psychology patterns.
Expected Output:
Design and train a Curator agent that extracts conversational patterns from successful interviews and builds a playbook of reusable strategies‚Äîwithout losing the nuance that makes them effective.

Technical Approach: Conversation structure analysis, pattern clustering, A/B testing across founder types, meta-learning for domain transfer.
3
Semantic Memory for an Evolving Market
Novel Challenge:
Existing knowledge graphs store static relationships. The Masters requires dynamic memory that captures how expert opinions evolve with evidence: "‚Ç¨50 is too expensive" ‚Üí sees demo ‚Üí "‚Ç¨50 is reasonable."
Expected Output:
Create a knowledge graph that models the validation process itself‚Äîtracking not just what experts believe, but why they believe it, what evidence changed their minds, and under what conditions opinions shift.

Technical Approach: Temporal knowledge graphs with timestamped edges, evidence linkage, belief confidence modeling, counterfactual reasoning.
Why This Matters: While existing ACE frameworks excel in structured domains (code, APIs, databases), our work addresses the novel challenge of unstructured, real-time human conversation‚Äîa significantly harder problem requiring fundamental research advances. Success here opens ACE applications to enterprise AI use cases currently considered impossible.
