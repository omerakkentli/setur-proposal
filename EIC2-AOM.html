Executive Synopsis

The Crisis: Despite 71% organizational penetration, generative AI initiatives face a 95% failure rate due to fundamental architectural misalignment between modern LLM capabilities and legacy enterprise systems. The current API-centric paradigm demands extensive backend reconfiguration, specialized Forward Deployed Engineers (costing $160k-$225k+ annually), and continuous infrastructure investment‚Äîcreating insurmountable barriers for EU enterprises, particularly SMBs.

"What if AI agents could learn by simply watching human workers‚Äîno API integration, no backend overhaul, no disruption?"

The Solution: Agentic Operational Mimicry (AOM) proposes a transformative solution: AI agents that learn directly from observing human workers through multimodal capture (screen, voice, interactions), autonomously deduce complex business logic, and execute tasks through existing user interfaces‚Äîeliminating the need for disruptive system overhauls.

üîë Key Innovation: This embodied AI approach positions the agent as a Digital Twin of operational talent, operating seamlessly within legacy IT landscapes while maintaining EU-compliant trustworthiness through explainable behavior, human oversight protocols, and algorithmic management safeguards.

The Paradigm Shift: By shifting the integration burden from enterprise backends to the AI agent itself, AOM addresses the single largest barrier to LLM adoption: workflow redesign friction. Where traditional solutions force enterprises to adapt their systems to AI, AOM adapts AI to work with existing systems.

Impact & Roadmap: Technical validation will advance from TRL 3 to TRL 6 across three phases, delivering a production-ready framework that enables non-invasive automation of knowledge-intensive processes, accelerates European digital transformation, and ensures AI benefits are realized responsibly at scale‚Äîdirectly supporting EU strategic goals for digital sovereignty, industrial modernization, and sustainable competitive advantage in accordance with AI Act mandates.
